The women who sang during the 1930's through the early 1950's represent a style of singing that was supplanted in later years by upbeat doo wop, pounding defiant rock and roll of the 1960's and 1970's and by the electronically modified techno pop of the 1980's.

Yet their sometimes sweet and sometimes sultry voices have lasted through the decades to influence the music of modern singers like Amy Winehouse and Adele.

Ella Fitzgerald

Born in Virginia in 1917, her musical career began in her teen years when she was introduced to bandleader Chick Webb and performed with him at the Savoy ballroom, recording over 150 songs with his band. She gained national fame with her 1938 breakout hit "A Tisket,A Tasket." She continued to perform for the majority of her life winning 13 Grammy's.

Dinah Washington

While still a child in elementary school, She sang gospel and played the piano for her church in Chicago eventually becoming the choir director in her teen years. At age 15, she won a local contest and got the opportunity to play in local jazz clubs with legendary musician Fats Waller. Her success continued into the 1950's crossing over into pop music. She was often critized as a sell out because she had the talent to sing in a variety of styles including blues, jazz, rhythm and blues and pop.

Sarah Vaughn

Born in Newark New Jersey, She won an amateur night contest at age 18 at the legendary Apollo that was a springboard to perform with many of the influential jazz musicians of the New York club scene. Her career lasted into the the late 1980's, including a performance at the White House for the Shah of Iran.
